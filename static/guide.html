Who Can Donate Blood?

Eligibility Criteria

You are eligible if:

  • Age: 18 – 65 years
  • Weight: Minimum 50 kg
  • Hemoglobin: Male ≥ 13 g/dL · Female ≥ 12.5 g/dL
  • Good general health with no recent illness
  • No recent medication that affects blood

You are NOT eligible if:

  • Recent surgery (within 6 months)
  • Pregnancy or recent childbirth
  • Active infection or fever
  • Recent tattoo or piercing (within 6 months)
  • Certain medications (blood thinners, etc.)
  • Recent blood donation (within 56 days)
  • HIV/AIDS, Hepatitis B/C, Malaria (permanent deferral)
  • Currently Menstruating (please wait until your menstrual cycle is complete before donating)
  • Low Hemoglobin During Menstruation (iron levels may be temporarily affected)
  • Weakness, Fatigue, or Dizziness (donation is recommended only when you feel fully well)

Who Can Donate Organs?

Organ Donation

Living Donation

  • Kidney — Healthy individual, 18-65 years
  • Part of Liver — Healthy donor, compatible blood type
  • Must be in excellent health
  • No major medical conditions
  • Must undergo full medical evaluation

Deceased Donation

  • Heart, Lungs, Liver, Kidneys
  • Pancreas, Intestines, Corneas, Skin
  • Donation does not depend on age
  • Medical condition determines suitability
  • Must have registered consent

How to Donate Blood

Process Guide
1
Registration Provide valid ID and donor details at the donation center
2
Health Screening Blood pressure, hemoglobin check, weight verification, and medical history review
3
Donation Actual blood draw takes 8–10 minutes. You'll donate approximately 450ml of blood
4
Refreshment & Recovery Rest for 15–20 minutes, enjoy light refreshments, and hydrate
5
Blood Processing Blood is separated into components (red cells, plasma, platelets) for different patients

Before Donation

Preparation
  • Sleep well — Get 7-8 hours of rest
  • Eat a healthy meal — Avoid fatty foods
  • Drink plenty of water — Stay hydrated
  • Carry valid ID proof — Aadhaar, Passport, or Driver's License
  • Avoid alcohol — 24 hours before donation
  • No smoking — 2 hours before donation
  • Wear comfortable clothes — Easy access to arms

After Donation

Recovery
  • Drink extra fluids — Water, juice, electrolyte drinks
  • Avoid heavy exercise — For 24 hours
  • Keep bandage on — For at least 4 hours
  • Eat iron-rich foods — Spinach, red meat, beans
  • Rest if feeling dizzy — Lie down and elevate legs
  • No alcohol — For 24 hours after donation
  • Remove bandage — After 4 hours, wash gently

Terms & Conditions

Important

1. Donor Consent

Donation is entirely voluntary. You must provide informed consent before any procedure. You have the right to withdraw consent at any time.

2. Medical Evaluation

All donors undergo rigorous medical screening to ensure safety for both donor and recipient. Results may affect eligibility.

3. Privacy Protection

Your personal and medical information is strictly confidential. Data is protected under national privacy laws and hospital policies.

4. Post-Donation Care

Medical staff will monitor you after donation. Follow all post-donation care instructions for your safety.

5. Organ Allocation

Organ donation follows national allocation guidelines. Matching is based on medical urgency, blood type compatibility, and organ size.
